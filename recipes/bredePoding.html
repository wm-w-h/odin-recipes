Lyode Soppes

Take Mylke an boyle it, an þanne take ȝolkys of eyroun y-tryid fro þe whyte, an draw hem þorwe A straynoure, an caste hem in-to þe mylke, an sette it on þe fyre an hete it, but let it nowt boyle; an stere it wyl tyl it be somwhat þikke; þenne caste þer-to Salt & Sugre, an kytte fayre paynemaynnys in round soppys, an caste þe soppys þer-on, an serue it forth for a potage.

INGREDIENTS

  • 3/4 C. milk
  • 1/4 C. heavy cream
  • 1 egg, or 2 egg yolks
  • 1-2 tbsp. sugar (to your taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Rounds of bread

DIRECTIONS

  1. Mix the heavy cream with the milk and add sugar, salt and egg.
  2. Place in a pot on the stove and cook over low heat until the mixture becomes thick. It is important that you keep the heat low because you don't want your custard to curdle.
  3. Meanwhile, put your round of bread into the bowl.
  4. When the custard has thickened, pour it through a strainer onto the bread.
